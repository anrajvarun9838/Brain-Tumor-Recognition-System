About Brain Tumors

Brain tumors occur when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in or around the brain. They may be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). The impact of a tumor depends on its type, size, location, and how quickly it grows.

The AI model in Brain Tumor Recognition System detects the following categories:

  • Glioma

    Originates from glial cells that support neurons. Gliomas are often found deep in brain tissue and can be low-grade or high-grade.

  • Meningioma

    Arises from the meninges (protective layers around the brain). Most meningiomas are benign and slow-growing.

  • Pituitary Tumor

    Develops in the pituitary gland and can affect hormone balance.

  • No Tumor Detected

    MRI appears normal with no abnormal growths identified.

Note: Brain Tumor Recognition System is a decision-support tool designed to assist medical professionals. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment.
